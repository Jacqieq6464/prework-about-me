Who Is Marvin Gaye?

Marvin Gaye was a brilliant singer & a songwriter with the Mowtown Record Label in the 1960s and 1970s. Like many singers in this period, he started singing at his father's church.

Popular Songs

Marvin is Responsible for some incredible, timeless songs. He was an amazing collaborator working with stars such as Diana Ross and Tammi Terrel. Marvin stopped all his collaboration work when his singing partner, Tammi, died of a brain tumour. Some of his many famous hits include:

Early Life

1939 - 1984

Marvin Gaye was born to Marvin Gay Snr and Alberta. His father was a church minister and his mother was a domestic worker. He had 5 siblings including his brothers from his mother's previous marriage and his father's extra-marital affairs.

He grew up in a troubled home and was frequently subjected to physical and emotional abuse at the hands of his father.

Throughout his childhood, Gaye often found peace in music, mastering the piano and drums at a young age. Until high school, his singing experience was limited to church revivals, but soon he developed a love for R&B and doo-wop that would set the foundation for his career. In the late 1950s, Gaye joined a vocal group called The New Moonglows.

Political Message

In 1970, inspired by escalating violence and political unrest over the Vietnam War, Gaye wrote the landmark song What's Going On. Despite clashes with Motown over the song's creative direction, the single was released in 1971 and became an instant smash. Its success prompted Gaye to take even more risks, both musically and politically. Death & Legacy

Despite his successful comeback in the early 1980s, Gaye struggled badly with the substance abuse and bouts of depression that had plagued him for most of his life. After his last tour, he moved into his parents' house. There he and his father fell into a pattern of violent fights and quarrels that recalled conflicts that had haunted the family for decades. On April 1, 1984, Marvin Gaye Sr. shot and killed his son after a physical altercation; the father claimed he acted in self-defence but would later be convicted of involuntary manslaughter.
